✦ France Beyond Paris

Our Destinations

Five magnificent cities, each with its own soul and story

Côte d'Azur

Nice

"The Queen of the French Riviera"

Nestled between the Mediterranean Sea and the foothills of the Alps, Nice enchants visitors with its stunning azure waters, vibrant markets, and belle-époque architecture. It's a city where art, nature, and the art of living beautifully converge.

🏛 Attractions: Promenade des Anglais, Castle Hill, Old Town (Vieux Nice), Matisse Museum, Marc Chagall National Museum
🍽 Local Food: Socca (chickpea flatbread), Salade Niçoise, Pissaladière (onion tart), Ratatouille
🎭 Festivals: Nice Carnival (one of the world's largest), Jazz Festival, Battle of Flowers
🏰 Landmarks: Cathédrale Saint-Nicolas, Place Masséna, Colline du Château ruins

Gastronomic Capital

Lyon

"The Culinary Heart of France"

Lyon, a UNESCO World Heritage city, is where history, gastronomy, and innovation intertwine. With its traboules (hidden passageways), silk-weaving heritage, and legendary bouchons, Lyon offers an authentic French experience unlike any other.

🏛 Attractions: Vieux Lyon (Old Town), Parc de la Tête d'Or, Place Bellecour, Confluence Museum, Traboules
🍽 Local Food: Quenelles de brochet, Andouillette, Praline tart, Cervelle de canut (herb cheese), Lyonnaise salad
🎭 Festivals: Fête des Lumières (Festival of Lights), Nuits de Fourvière, Nuits Sonores
🏰 Landmarks: Basilica of Notre-Dame de Fourvière, Roman amphitheatres, Cathédrale Saint-Jean-Baptiste

Mediterranean Port City

Marseille

"France's Gateway to the Mediterranean"

As France's oldest and most multicultural city, Marseille pulses with an energy that's both ancient and refreshingly modern. From the dramatic Calanques coastline to the bustling Vieux-Port fish market, this is a city that engages all the senses.

🏛 Attractions: Vieux-Port, Calanques National Park, MuCEM Museum, Le Panier neighborhood, Château d'If
🍽 Local Food: Bouillabaisse (fish stew), Navettes (orange-blossom cookies), Panisse (chickpea fries), Pastis
🎭 Festivals: Fiesta des Suds, Festival de Marseille, Carnival of Marseille
🏰 Landmarks: Notre-Dame de la Garde, Abbaye Saint-Victor, Fort Saint-Jean, Palais Longchamp

Wine Capital of the World

Bordeaux

"Where Wine and Elegance Flow Together"

Bordeaux is synonymous with exceptional wine, but this elegant city offers so much more. Its perfectly preserved 18th-century architecture earned it UNESCO World Heritage status, while the modern Cité du Vin puts it at the forefront of wine culture tourism.

🏛 Attractions: Place de la Bourse, Miroir d'Eau, Cité du Vin, Rue Sainte-Catherine, Darwin Ecosystem
🍽 Local Food: Canelés (rum pastry), Entrecôte bordelaise, Oysters from Arcachon Bay, Dune du Pilat
🎭 Festivals: Bordeaux Wine Festival, Fête le vin, River Festival, Bordeaux Open Air music festival
🏰 Landmarks: Cathédrale Saint-André, Grand Théâtre, Pont de Pierre, Porte Cailhau

Franco-German Heritage

Strasbourg

"A Fairytale at the Crossroads of Europe"

Strasbourg is where France and Germany embrace in a charming cultural fusion. Its iconic Petite France district, with half-timbered houses reflected in canals, feels like stepping into a storybook. As the seat of the European Parliament, it represents the heart of European unity.

🏛 Attractions: Petite France, European Parliament, Orangerie Park, Strasbourg Astronomical Clock, Covered Bridges
🍽 Local Food: Tarte flambée (Flammkuchen), Choucroute garnie, Kougelhopf cake, Bredele cookies
🎭 Festivals: Christkindelsmärik (Christmas Market — oldest in France!), Festival Musica, Strasbourg Mon Amour
🏰 Landmarks: Cathédrale Notre-Dame de Strasbourg, Palais Rohan, Barrage Vauban, Maison Kammerzell
